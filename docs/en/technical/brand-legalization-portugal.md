## 1. Brand Name: "Du_arte ☥ rawmesh"

**Can you use it?** Yes — with some caveats.

### Check availability

**INPI (Instituto Nacional da Propriedade Industrial — the Portuguese Patent and Trademark Office)**

- Search: <https://servicosonline.inpi.pt/pesquisas/main/marcas.jsp>
- Search for "Du_arte", "Duarte", "rawmesh"
- If no identical or similar mark is registered, you can proceed

**Empresa.Online (commercial name registry)**

- <https://eportugal.gov.pt>
- Check whether the company name is available

### Protect the brand

**Trademark registration with INPI:** ~€300–400 (valid for 10 years)

**Relevant Nice classes:**

- **Class 35:** Business services
- **Class 40:** 3D printing and material treatment
- **Class 25:** If you sell t-shirts or merchandise

⚠️ **Note:** The ☥ (Ankh) symbol cannot be officially registered with INPI — special symbols aren't accepted in legal names. But you can use it as:

- **Visual commercial name:** "Du_arte rawmesh" (legal)
- **Logo / branding:** "Du_arte ☥ rawmesh" (visual only)

## 2. Legal Structure — What to Open?

### Option 1: Self-Employed (Recibos Verdes) ← Recommended starting point

*"Recibos Verdes" — literally "green receipts" — is the Portuguese self-employed / freelancer tax regime.*

- ✅ Simpler and cheaper
- ✅ No upfront costs
- ✅ You pay IRS (Portuguese personal income tax) on profits (23% in the base bracket)
- ✅ Social Security: ~21.4% of income
- ❌ Unlimited liability (your personal assets are on the line)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Setup cost** | €0 |
| **Time** | 1 day (online) |
| **Suggested CAE code** | 25620 (Machining) |

*CAE = Classificação Portuguesa das Atividades Económicas, the Portuguese economic activity classification code.*

### Option 2: Single-Shareholder Limited Company (Unipessoal por Quotas) ← When you scale up

- ✅ Separates personal assets from the business
- ✅ More professional
- ❌ Costs: ~€360 to incorporate + monthly accountant fees (€50–150/month)
- ❌ More bureaucracy

| | |
|---|---|
| **Setup cost** | ~€360 + accountant |
| **Time** | 2–5 days |

## 3. Steps to Get Legal (Self-Employed Route)

### Step 1: Register the activity with the Tax Authority (Finanças)

- **Portal das Finanças:** <https://www.portaldasfinancas.gov.pt>
- Submit the Declaração de Início de Atividade (Activity Start Declaration)
- **CAE code:** 25620 (Machining) or 74100 (Specialized design)
- **Cost:** €0

### Step 2: Social Security Registration

- Automatic after registering with Finanças
- You pay ~€70–100/month (depending on income)
- **Cost:** €0 (you only pay the monthly contribution afterwards)

### Step 3: Invoicing

- You need to issue invoices and receipts
- **Free software:** InvoiceXpress (free up to a certain limit) or Moloni
- Or paper invoices (not recommended)

### Step 4: VAT (IVA)

*IVA = Imposto sobre o Valor Acrescentado, the Portuguese VAT.*

- If you bill **less than €13,500/year:** EXEMPT from IVA (simplified regime)
- If you bill **more than €13,500/year:** charge 23% IVA and remit it to the State

## 5. Legal Protection — How NOT to Get Your Brand Stolen

### ✅ Register the trademark with INPI (€300–400)

- Protects "Du_arte" or "Du arte rawmesh" (without ☥ in the official registration)
- Valid 10 years, renewable

### ✅ Web domain (optional)

- Register `duarte-rawmesh.pt` or similar
- **Cost:** ~€10/year

### ✅ CC BY-SA 4.0 license (already in place)

- For content, designs, and pages
- Protects authorship while permitting sharing

### ✅ Service agreement contract

- For larger clients
- Defines deliverables, deadlines, and payment terms
